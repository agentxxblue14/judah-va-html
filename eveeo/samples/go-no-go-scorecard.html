Sample scorecard

Project Go or No-Go Scorecard

Use: every incoming charging or inspection lead, before any quote · Decision owner: Strategic PM → Leadership
This is the paper version of the live gate on the pitch page, built to show how I would standardize EVEEO's "should we pursue this?" call. The weights below are a sensible starting point. The first thing I would do on the job is recalibrate them against your historical win/loss data.

1. How it works

  1. Score each factor 0, 1, or 2 (0 = fail, 1 = caution, 2 = clear).
  2. Two factors are critical. A 0 on either is an automatic NO-GO regardless of total.
  3. Otherwise compare the weighted total against the thresholds in section 3.

2. Scoring grid

FactorWeightWhat a 2 (clear) looks like
Utility grid capacity CRITICALx2Feeder supports the load with no major upgrade
Margin after hardware & transformer CRITICALx2Profit holds once interconnection costs land
Zoning & permittingx1Use is permitted, no variance fight
Hardware lead time vs client deadlinex1Equipment arrives inside the timeline
Fit with EVEEO track recordx1We have executed this profile before
Operational capacity to staff itx1Crew and PM bandwidth available on schedule

Maximum weighted score: 16. (Inspection-program leads swap the EV-specific rows for diagnostic tooling, lane throughput, and compliance readiness, same scoring logic.)

3. Decision thresholds

GOBoth critical factors clear and total at or above 13. Build full scope and proposal.
HOLDCriticals clear but total 9 to 12. Resolve the weak factors before quoting.
NO-GOAny critical scored 0, or total below 9. Decline and protect the team's time.

4. Worked example (illustrative numbers)

FactorScoreWeighted
Utility grid capacity24
Margin after costs12
Zoning & permitting22
Hardware lead time11
Track record fit22
Operational capacity22
Total13

Verdict: GO, with a watch. Criticals clear and total hits 13, so it is worth a proposal. Margin scored only 1, so the PM flags it: tighten the interconnection cost estimate before the number goes to the client.

5. Why this matters

The scorecard turns a gut call into a defensible, repeatable decision leadership can trust, and it creates a paper trail. Over time the scored leads become the historical data that sharpens the weights.
