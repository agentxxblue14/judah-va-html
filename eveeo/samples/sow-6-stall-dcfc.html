Statement of Work (excerpt): 6-Stall DC Fast Charging Site

Prepared by: EVEEO · Client: [Retail property owner] · Status: sample / illustrative
A short excerpt to show how I scope and write an SOW that protects EVEEO from scope creep. The structure (clear inclusions, explicit exclusions, named assumptions, acceptance criteria) is the part that prevents disputes later. Figures are illustrative placeholders, not a real quote.

1. Project summary

EVEEO will design, permit, install, and commission six DC Fast Charging stalls on the Client's retail lot, delivered turnkey from site assessment through energization and operations handover.

2. Scope of work (inclusions)

  1. Site assessment, electrical load study, and utility interconnection coordination.
  2. Permitting and zoning submissions, including ADA-compliant stall layout.
  3. Supply of six DCFC dispensers and associated switchgear per the agreed spec.
  4. Civil and electrical installation, including any transformer work identified in the load study.
  5. Network configuration, pricing setup, and load management.
  6. Commissioning per EVEEO SOP 04 and operations handover with documentation.

3. Inclusions vs exclusions

Included

  • Hardware, install, commissioning
  • Permit fees up to the stated cap
  • 90 days post-go-live support

Explicitly excluded

  • Utility upgrade costs beyond the load-study estimate
  • Site lighting, canopies, or paving
  • Ongoing network subscription after month 3

4. Key assumptions (each one is a risk if false)

  • Existing service capacity is sufficient or upgradable within the estimated cost.
  • Permits are issued within the standard regional timeline.
  • Client provides site access and clears parking during install windows.
  • Hardware lead times hold; supply-chain slips are handled by change order.

5. Timeline (illustrative)

PhaseDurationMilestone
Assessment & design2 to 3 weeksApproved design and load study
Permitting4 to 8 weeksPermits issued (regional dependent)

6. Acceptance criteria

  • All six stalls pass the commissioning tests in SOP 04 with zero open faults for 24 hours.
  • Network reporting confirmed; pricing and access live.
  • As-built documentation and warranty contacts delivered to the Client.

7. Change control

Any work outside sections 2 and 3, or any assumption in section 4 proving false, is handled by a written change order with revised cost and schedule before work proceeds. This is the clause that keeps the project profitable.
