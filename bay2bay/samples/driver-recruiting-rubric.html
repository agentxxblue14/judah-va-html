Driver Recruiting & First-Round Screening

Pipeline: post ad → review applications → first-round screen → schedule qualified candidates for you
This is how I would keep your driver pipeline full without eating your time. A consistent rubric means only qualified, scheduled candidates reach you. The questions and scoring below are a starting set I would tune to Bay2Bay's standards.

1. Application review (pass / fail gate)

  • Valid driver license and clean enough record for your insurance
  • Able to do the physical work (lifting) and the schedule
  • Reliable vehicle access to get to the yard, and a working phone
  • Auto-screen out: no license info, no-shows on prior history flagged, unwilling on core requirements

2. First-round screen questions

  1. Walk me through your driving and delivery experience.
  2. How do you handle a tight delivery window when traffic or a breakdown hits?
  3. Tell me about a time you had a vehicle issue on the road. What did you do?
  4. How do you keep your receipts and paperwork straight day to day?
  5. What hours and days can you commit to, and how long are you looking to stay?

3. Scoring rubric (1 to 3 each, schedule if 11+ and no red flags)

4. What reaches you

Only candidates who pass the gate and score 11 or higher, already scheduled, with my one-line note on each. You spend your time on final calls, not sorting applications.
