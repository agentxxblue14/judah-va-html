Step 1 · who actually buys founder leads

The buyer map: every lead type matched to the firms that need it

I don't message "business owners." I match each lead category you collect to the specific firms whose pipeline depends on reaching founders. Each row below is a separate Sales Navigator search and a separate message angle.

Hiring leads
Recruiters
Recruitment & staffing agencies, RPO firms, fractional-talent platforms, embedded-recruiting consultants. They spend all day prospecting for companies that are hiring. A founder who said "I need to hire" is their highest-intent lead.
Sales Nav: title = "Founder/Director, Recruitment" OR "Talent Partner" · industry = Staffing & Recruiting · geography = Malaysia + SEA + remote-global · 2–50 employees
Capital leads
Capital intros
Fundraising advisors, capital-introduction firms, angel-network leads, debt and invoice-finance brokers, fractional CFOs. Their business is connecting founders to money and taking a fee. A founder actively raising is exactly who they pay to find.
Sales Nav: title = "Fundraising Advisor" OR "Capital Introduction" OR "Fractional CFO" · keywords = "founders / startups / SME finance"
Exit leads
Brokers & M&A
Business brokers, boutique M&A advisors, exit-planning consultants. They live or die on deal flow. A founder thinking about selling is the start of a six-figure mandate for them.
Sales Nav: title = "Business Broker" OR "M&A Advisor" OR "Exit Planning" · industry = Financial Services / Investment Banking · SME-focused
Growth leads
Agencies & CMOs
Marketing and growth agencies, fractional CMOs, paid-media and SEO shops. A founder who said "I need to grow" is a warm retainer waiting to happen, and they normally pay cold-outreach VAs to find exactly that.
Sales Nav: title = "Founder, Agency" OR "Fractional CMO" OR "Head of Growth" · industry = Marketing & Advertising · 2–30 employees
Service leads
Ops & advisory
Fractional COOs, ops consultants, legal and accounting service providers, BPO and outsourcing firms. Broad demand, lower intent, best used as JV partners who both buy leads and refer their own founders into your community.
Sales Nav: title = "Fractional COO" OR "Management Consultant" OR "Founder, BPO" · cross-reference with your existing community members
Step 2 · the 5-touch sequence

How a stranger becomes a booked call, in real copy

No pitch in the connection request, ever. Value and proof before the ask. This is the recruiter-buyer version; every variable in mint is personalized by hand off the person's profile and recent activity. The JV version swaps touch 4 for a partnership angle.

Day 0Connection requestget accepted, zero pitch
Hi [First name], I keep seeing your posts on [specific topic they post about] and the SME hiring angle lines up with a founder community I work with here in Malaysia. Would be glad to connect.
Under LinkedIn's note limit, references something real on their profile, sounds like a person, not a funnel.
On acceptOpen the loopcontext, still no hard sell
Thanks for connecting, [First name]. Quick context: I help run a private founder community where owners come to us when they need to hire, raise, or grow. We get more "I need to hire" conversations than we can use, and I thought a firm like [their company] might actually want those. Open to me sending one real example so you can see the quality?
Ends on a low-friction yes/no. The "want me to send an example" frame makes the next message feel invited, not pushed.
Day 3The proofshare the sample lead you provide
Here's a sanitized one from this week: [founder lead detail you give me — sector, size, the exact need, timeline]. This is the standard, not the exception. If a steady flow of these would be useful, worth a quick call to see what a fit looks like?
This is where your sample-lead material does the selling. My job is to land it in front of the right person with the right framing.
Day 7The askbook the call
No rush, [First name]. If it's useful, I can set up 15 minutes with [your name / the team] to walk through volume, categories, and how the handoff works. Here's the link: [Calendly]. If now's not the time, I'll check back rather than keep nudging.
Direct CTA with a scheduling link. The "I'll check back rather than nudge" line lowers resistance and earns the follow-up.
Day 14Break-up / JV pivotlast value, or convert to partner
Last note from me, [First name]. If buying leads isn't the model, some firms prefer a JV: you take our founders, we send each other warm intros, no upfront. If that's more your speed, happy to explore it. Either way, glad we connected.
Break-up messages get strong reply rates, and the JV offer turns a "no" on buying into a possible long-term partner.
Step 3 · don't get the account banned

Volume that scales without tripping LinkedIn's limits

The fastest way to kill this channel is to blast it. I run conservative, warmed limits so the account stays healthy for months, not weeks. This is the part most outreach VAs ignore until the profile is restricted.

CONNECTS

20–25 / day

Roughly 100 a week, the safe ceiling for a non-premium account. New or cold accounts start lower and ramp over 2–3 weeks.

MESSAGES

30–50 / day

Follow-ups to existing connections, spread across the day, never in one burst that looks automated.

WARM-UP

Profile first

Optimized profile, a few real posts, light engagement before outreach, so requests come from a credible human, not a ghost.

No mass-automation tools that risk a ban Manual personalization on every first touch Withdraw stale requests to keep accept-rate clean One account healthy beats three accounts burned
