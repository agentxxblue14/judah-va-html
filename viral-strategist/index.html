The lens

The brain decides in under a second. I build for that.

Before anyone consciously chooses, the brain runs three checks. A scroll-stopper answers all three at once. Everything below is built to pass them.

CHECK 1

Have I seen this and liked it?

Familiarity beats originality. I start from a format the brain already trusts, then put a fresh concept inside it.

CHECK 2

Is something unexpected?

A pattern interrupt in the first half second. The curiosity gap is the reason the thumb stops.

CHECK 3

Does this involve me?

Identity and emotion. People share what makes them look good, so the share is designed in, not hoped for.

Zach King, "Magic Broomstick"

TikTok · @zachking · engine: mechanical curiosity
~2.2B
views (most-viewed TikTok)
Hook
First frame is mid-action on a broom. Instant Harry Potter recognition plus "is he actually going to fly?"
Curiosity gap
Mechanical, not narrative: you know the payoff, you cannot predict how it's done in one shot. It survives rewatching.
Emotional arc
Anticipation, delight, disbelief. Ends on a surprise spike, the emotion most tied to sharing.
Pacing
~7 seconds, one invisible cut at the peak, zero filler. The whole video protects one reveal.
Identity appeal
Sharing says "I have taste in clever craft." It makes you look delighted, not fooled.
Why shared
"How did he do that" forces a rewatch and a tag. Looking good plus a puzzle.
Story structure
Hook (on a broom), Problem (can a real person fly?), Story (the run-up), Payoff (seamless lift-off).
The skeleton
Ordinary setup, then one impossible seamless reveal.
Rebuilt with confidence energy

Plain clothes in front of a closet, one jump, land mid-stride in a full transformed outfit on a city street. One invisible cut on the beat drop. Same mechanical "how" gap, same surprise spike, brand new topic, and the track's momentum carries the run-up into the reveal.

Hilton, "It Matters Where You Stay"

Instagram Reels · @hilton (Deepika Padukone) · engine: recognition into relief
~5B
views (most-viewed reel, a branded ad)
Hook
Opens like every influencer travel reel, then immediately mocks that format. Familiar, but subverted.
Curiosity gap
By breaking the polished-travel cliche early, it makes you watch to see how far the self-aware joke goes.
Emotional arc
Recognition, amusement, relief. It validates a shared frustration with fake travel perfection.
Pacing
Quick setup-punchline beats. Long enough to land the bit, tight enough to rewatch.
Identity appeal
Sharing says "I'm in on the joke, not fooled by fake perfection." An in-group signal.
Why shared
Shared identity (smart traveler) plus it makes the sharer look self-aware, not sold-to.
Story structure
Hook (familiar reel), Problem (travel content is fake), Story (the takedown), Payoff (brand as the honest answer).
The skeleton
Set up a familiar aspirational format, subvert it, deliver the real payoff. A boring brand going viral via a stolen format, exactly Tuan Le's thesis.
Rebuilt with galasalah rendez vous

A dreamy "perfect morning routine" reel scored by the track that keeps getting interrupted by real life (alarm fails, coffee spills), then resolves into one genuinely calm moment. The aspirational music makes the fantasy land harder, the subversion makes it relatable and shareable.

Tariq, "It's Corn" (Corn Kid)

TikTok and YouTube · song version · engine: wholesome identity
10s of M
views (approx, plus duets and stitches)
Hook
A kid's unfiltered, total joy in the first second. Pure emotion is rare on the feed, so the brain flags it as novel and safe.
Curiosity gap
Softer and emotional: "who is this kid and why is he this happy about corn?"
Emotional arc
Surprise, warmth, delight. A clean hit of wholesome serotonin with zero cynicism.
Pacing
The autotuned song turns a talking clip into a loop. The musical hook is the rewatch engine.
Identity appeal
Sharing says "I'm wholesome, I appreciate genuine joy." It makes the sharer look kind and fun.
Why shared
Identity and emotion, plus it became participatory: everyone made their own "it's ___" version.
Story structure
Hook (a beaming kid), Problem (will it stay this pure?), Story (his sincere love), Payoff (a line you cannot unhear).
The skeleton
One person's sincere, specific, un-self-conscious joy, turned into a singable, remixable loop.
Rebuilt with kumglatchko give

A montage of regular people naming the small ordinary thing they're irrationally happy about (a perfectly toasted bagel, a clean desk), cut to the playful bounce of the track, ending on a repeatable tagline that invites duets. Same wholesome-identity engine, same make-your-own shareability, new subject.
