Sample 1 · guest-issue triage SOP

Every issue sorted by how much it hurts the stay

The mistake is treating a lockout and a flickering bulb the same way. I sort by guest impact first, so the urgent things move now and the rest get batched. Tiers and targets below are a starting point to agree with the team.

P1
Urgent

Guest blocked or unsafe

Can't access the property, no power, water or heat, gas smell, security concern, a booking that won't let them check in.
Acknowledge by phone within [X] min · fix or escalate to on-call trades / manager immediately · do not let it sit overnight
P2
Comfort

Stay degraded but liveable

Aircon or heating down, intermittent hot water, key appliance out, no wifi, cleanliness issue found on arrival.
Acknowledge within [X] min · dispatch same day · offer an interim fix and set a clear expectation with the guest
P3
Routine

Minor or owner admin

Small defects, a bulb, a chip in paint, restock requests, non-urgent owner questions, compliance paperwork.
Log it · batch into the next scheduled visit or weekly admin block · never lose it
1 Acknowledge→ 2 Diagnose→ 3 Act or dispatch→ 4 Follow through→ 5 Close loop with guest→ 6 Log + note for owner

Sample 5 · the workflow gap I'd close first

One automation that removes a daily manual job

Your post asks for someone who spots smarter processes. Here's a concrete one, built from tools you already use.

Booking change → clean reschedules itself

Right now a cancellation or date change usually means someone remembers to move the clean, ping the cleaner, and update the calendar. Miss it and you pay for a clean on an empty night, or a guest walks into a dirty property.

Zapier: booking modified or cancelled in [PMS / Homhero]  →  cleaning task auto-reschedules in Breezeway  →  cleaner gets a Slack / WhatsApp alert  →  calendar and ops board update  →  flag to me only if it can't auto-resolve.

Net effect: fewer wasted cleans, no guest walking into a missed turnover, and one less thing a coordinator has to hold in their head.
